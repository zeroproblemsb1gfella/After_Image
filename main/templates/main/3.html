IT'S SUCH A BEAUTIFUL DAY

Directed, Written, Animated, Photographed, and Produced by Don Hertzfeldt.

"It's Such a Beautiful Day" is an animated film that tells the story of Bill, a stick figure with a black top hat who is struggling with a severe mental illness that causes him to experience strange and terrifying hallucinations. The movie utilizes both offbeat humor and profound philosophical musings.

STYLE

Throughout the film, Hertzfeldt uses a unique animation style that blends traditional hand-drawn animation with experimental techniques like stop-motion, time-lapse photography, and live-action footage. The result is a visually stunning and emotionally powerful film that explores themes of mental illness, family, and the human life.

AWARDS

Don Hertzfeldt's "It's Such a Beautiful Day" has been critically acclaimed, and received many accolades including being the runner-up for The Los Angeles Film Critics Association's "Best Animated Feature Film", being ranked #1 in The Film Stage's "Best Animated Films of the 21st Century", named #1 by The Wrap for "Best Animated Film of the 2010s", and #1 in IGN's "Top 10 Animated Films of All Time" list. The film has been described as "one of the great outsider artworks of the modern era, at once sympathetic and shocking, beautiful and horrifying, angry and hilarious, uplifting and almost unbearably sad"
