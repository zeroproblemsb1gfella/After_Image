Rules of The Game

Directed By: Jean Renoir

Starring: Marcel Dalio as Marquis | Nora Gregor as Christine | Paulette Dubost as Lisette

Rules of The Game is a French film that highlights the life of the upper class in France before WWII. It plays out in a country estate during a party consisting of only the rich. Inside the party is where any confusing love affairs, and social games take place. It showcases the greed and hipocracy that comes out of the upper-class.

Controversy

When Rules of The Game came out, it was extremely controversial as it critized the upper class like never seen before. It was banned in France on release, but is still considered one of the greatest films ever made.

The film successfully tells stories of multiple cast members throughout the film overlapping perfectly together. It creates a unique complex, and in depth experience for the watcher. The film allows for the watchers to see difference and interactions between difference social classes.
