Extended Information:

The entire film from a visual perspective is quite literally a blue screen. There is a heavy emphasis on audiowork and voiceovers. Within the film’s voiceover, it is spoken by Jarman and other collaborators in the form of Tilda Swinton, Nigel Terry and John Quentin. The text is often spoken in a form of verse and is augmented by music and sound by composer Simon Fisher-Turner, Coil, Momus, Karol Szymanowski and Eric Satie.Towards the end of 1992 when Jarman has come back to Blue in his original thoughts,he wanted a blue screen that was completely devoid of imagery so that “the admirable austerity of the void” would not be undermined. Jarman had then explained in a late proposal for the film: “The monochrome is an alchemy, effective liberation from personality. It articulates silence. It is a fragment of an immense work without limit. The blue of the landscape of liberty.”
