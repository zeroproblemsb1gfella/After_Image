Interesting Facts

Trivia
Many movies todays are reliant on green screen and CGI, however Tom Cruise insisted to keep that to a minimum. Aerial shots were captured by inflight and on ground cameras, while the acrobatics were performed by real pilots, even the up close cockpit shots were taken during flights to capture the casts expression. This meant the casts had to undergo real-life pilot training to prepare for this experince. This combination provided the audience as close to real-life experience, a ride along.

According to Miles Teller, the actor of Rooster, son of Goose from the previous movie, the cast got to choose their own call signs. Miles Teller chose Rooster as it is in the same family as his fathers call sign.

According to Miles Teller, many casts threw up during inflight filming, as many of the shots taken were real. Casts experienced lots of G-forces during filming.

During the cockpit sceens, the cast had to film themselves, taking advice from Tom Cruise, everyone had to know when to cut, adjust lighting, handle thier own sound. Footage could not be reviewed until the actors have landed, if shots werent up to standard, actors would have to take off again and refilm.
Movie Errors
Plot holes
Admiral Cain in the earlier part of the film disregarded the needs for pilots as technology has brought unmanned aerial vehicles (regarded as drones), no more need to risk human lives. However, this was no mentioned during the rest of the movie, espically during the final mission, seems like an oversight.
Errors
As pointed out by real fighter jet pilots in the USAF, the SAMs that were shooting missles at the four protagonist planes in the final fight, should of blew up and damanged the protagonist, even if the missles does not directly hit, as SAM missles are detonated via proximity, not direct hit.

Since the mission target was guarded by enemy radar, aircrafts, and SAM sites, the pilots would of had a better fighting chance in using a 5th Gen fighter to level the playing field, or even the navy using UAVs to prevent risking the lives of their pilots. Futher more, the same Tomahawk cruise missiles used to destroy the enemy airbase could of destroy the mission target as well. The choice to send pilots using a older generation fighter is questionable at best.
